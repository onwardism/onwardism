# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Onwardism community pledge to make participation in our platform and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on advancing knowledge in cloud computing, data engineering, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

**Technical Excellence**
- Sharing accurate, well-researched technical content
- Providing constructive feedback on technical implementations
- Acknowledging sources and giving proper attribution
- Correcting mistakes promptly and transparently

**Community Engagement**
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

**Professional Conduct**
- Maintaining professional standards in all interactions
- Respecting intellectual property and licensing terms
- Following platform guidelines and terms of service
- Supporting newcomers and junior engineers

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

**Harassment and Discrimination**
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Discriminatory language or actions based on protected characteristics

**Technical Misconduct**
- Deliberately sharing false or misleading technical information
- Plagiarism or failure to properly attribute sources
- Spamming or excessive self-promotion
- Sharing malicious code or security vulnerabilities irresponsibly

**Platform Abuse**
- Attempting to circumvent platform security measures
- Creating fake accounts or impersonating others
- Manipulating voting, engagement, or ranking systems
- Violating terms of service or community guidelines

## Content Standards

### Technical Content

**Quality Requirements**
- Content must be technically accurate and well-researched
- Include proper citations and references for claims
- Provide practical value to the engineering community
- Follow established writing and formatting guidelines

**Prohibited Content**
- Malicious code or instructions for harmful activities
- Copyrighted material without proper licensing
- Misleading or deliberately false technical information
- Content that violates applicable laws or regulations

### Community Discussions

**Constructive Engagement**
- Focus discussions on technical merit and factual accuracy
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Acknowledge when you don't know something
- Be open to learning from others' expertise

**Professional Discourse**
- Maintain respectful tone even during disagreements
- Avoid personal attacks or ad hominem arguments
- Stay on topic and relevant to the technical discussion
- Respect different approaches to solving technical problems

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Moderation Actions

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate
- Communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

**Platform Areas**
- Published articles, blogs, and news content
- Comments and discussions on content
- User profiles and public information
- Direct messages and private communications on the platform

**External Spaces**
- Official social media accounts
- Community events and meetups
- Conference presentations representing Onwardism
- Other online or offline venues when representing the community

## Reporting

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Primary Contact**: conduct@onwardism.com
**Alternative Contact**: community@onwardism.com
**Anonymous Reporting**: Available through platform reporting tools

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:
- Description of the incident
- Date, time, and location (if applicable)
- Names of individuals involved
- Screenshots or evidence (if available)
- Impact on you or the community
- Any previous related incidents

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: We will acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: Thorough investigation of the reported incident
3. **Decision**: Determination of appropriate response
4. **Action**: Implementation of corrective measures
5. **Follow-up**: Check-in with reporter on resolution

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

All enforcement actions may be appealed through:
- **Email**: appeals@onwardism.com
- **Timeline**: Appeals must be submitted within 30 days
- **Process**: Independent review by different community leaders
- **Response**: Decision communicated within 14 days

### Appeal Considerations

Appeals will be considered based on:
- New evidence not available during initial investigation
- Procedural errors in the enforcement process
- Disproportionate consequences relative to the violation
- Demonstrated understanding and commitment to improvement
